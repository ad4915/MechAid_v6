Welcome to MechAid!

About...

MechAid was founded in 2019, and was designed to help mechanical engineering students at Imperial by providing them with knowledge accessible to all students outside of what they are informed of through the college staff. The rationale behind this is to create something that is student based, as the experience that senior students who have just been through the process can often be much more informative and valuable than what you can find online or by speaking to members of staff. Although the ‘Mums and Dads’ scheme attempts to provide this, students often lose touch with their ‘parents’ or may be allocated unsupportive ‘parents’ or ‘parents’ who are unable to offer the right advice. Through the creation of a website, all students within the department will have access to the knoweldge provided and will have access to it at anytime.

The content of this website focuses on two key areas which are careers advice and guidance on the mechanical engineering degree. Securing internships and jobs was identified as a weakness within the department as this was found to be something that many mechanical engineering students struggle with. This would largely be due to their lack of knowledge and guidance. The mechanical engineering degree at Imperial is inherently extremely tough and stressful. Making the right choices during your degree is therefore fundamental, to make it less stressful, more manageable and enjoyable. As such the section on guidance on the degree was created that provides knowlege to students to ensure they don't make mistakes that they regret along the way.

What We Offer...


Consultancy

Consultancy has become an ever-increasingly popular mechanical engineering graduate path, and this guide will help you land your dream job in this sector. Be it anything from strategy consultancy at McKinsey to techincal consultancy at Newton, this section will be of benefit to any aspiring consultants.

Engineering

Despite being mechanical engineers at Imperial, this industry is by no means easy to get into, especially if you are seeking the top jobs out there. Whether you are looking for a traditional job at Rolls Royce, or something more creative at Cambridge Consultants, don't miss out on this guide where you will discover everything from portfolio building to assessment centre questions.

Investment Banking

This section provides a comprehensive guide to how to break into what is one of the most competive industries to get in. With step-by-step instructions and tricks and tips that have been discovered the hard way, your route to an investment banking job will be much easier and with a greater chance of success!



PhDs

If you're keen to secure a PhD, then this guide will teach you the necessary steps. From selecting the right final year project and networking with the correct members of staff to deadlines and funding applications, this guide is extensive!

CVs & Cover Letters

This section provide niche advice regarding writing these application documents, with unique information that you will not find easily elsewhere. Your current ones will likely change significantly after completing this section, and you will develop an impeccable CV and cover letter!

Module Selection Info

If you're in ME2 or ME3, you will have to pick five optional modules to undertake next year. These modules may define your overall expereince of the year, as well as opening up a range of final year projects for ME4. Making the correct choices is therefore essential, and this section provides you with an exhaustive list of everything you should consider when deciding.
